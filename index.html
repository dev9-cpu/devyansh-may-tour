Badrinath-Temple

The Badrinath Temple is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India, situated in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, one of the principal deities in Hinduism, and forms part of the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit, along with Yamunotri, Gangotri, and Kedarnath.

Kedarnath-Temple

The Kedarnath Temple is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India, located in the Uttarakhand region, and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, which also include Badrinath, 4 Yamunotri, and Gangotri. Kedarnath holds a special place in Hinduism, especially among devotees of Lord Shiva.

Vrindavan-Temple

Vrindavan is a town in the Mathura district of Uttar Pradesh, India, renowned for its association with Lord Krishna. It is home to several temples dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha, marking it as one of the most important pilgrimage sites for followers of Hinduism, particularly those who follow the Vaishnavism tradition.
